If you're in Tokyo and looking for a quick getaway, there are fantastic day trips to explore. From the historic temples of Kamakura to the stunning views of Mount Fuji, each destination offers its own unique experiences. Here’s my take on the best spots to visit.

Why You Should Visit Kamakura

Kamakura is a charming coastal town with rich history. Just about one hour from Tokyo by train, it costs around ¥940 one way. When you arrive, you’ll feel like you’ve stepped into another world with the sound of ocean waves and temple bells.

Here’s what you shouldn’t miss: - Great Buddha (Daibutsu): This iconic bronze statue stands at 13.35 meters tall and is a sight to behold. - Hase-dera Temple: Known for its beautiful gardens and stunning views of the coast, it’s a peaceful spot to unwind. - Komachi Street: The perfect place to grab some snacks and shop for souvenirs. Try the sweet potato ice cream; it’s delicious!

As Kay puts it, “Kamakura feels like stepping into another world with the sound of ocean waves and temple bells.” Trust me, you’ll love it.

Exploring Nikko: A UNESCO World Heritage Site

If you’re a history buff or a nature lover, Nikko is your destination. Located about two hours north of Tokyo, it’s home to the UNESCO World Heritage site, Toshogu Shrine.

Here’s what to do in Nikko: 1. Toshogu Shrine: Marvel at the intricate carvings and the famous Yomeimon Gate. 2. Kegon Falls: Standing at 100 meters, it’s one of Japan’s top three waterfalls. The view is breathtaking! 3. Lake Chuzenji: A beautiful spot for a boat ride or a picnic.

Nikko is a fantastic mix of spirituality and nature, making it a perfect day trip.

Is Hakone Worth the Trip from Tokyo?

Now let’s talk about Hakone. If you’re looking for stunning views and hot springs, this is the place. A day trip to Mount Fuji typically costs between $150 and $190 per person, which includes transportation and activities.

What to expect in Hakone: - Onsen (hot springs): Relax in one of the many onsens. I recommend the Yunessun Spa Resort for a unique experience. - Lake Ashi: Take a scenic boat ride with views of Mount Fuji. It’s absolutely stunning! - Hakone Open-Air Museum: This outdoor museum features incredible sculptures and art installations surrounded by nature.

Hakone is definitely worth your time if you want a mix of relaxation and adventure.

What to Do in Yokohama on a Day Trip

Yokohama is a vibrant city with plenty to explore, and it’s just a short trip from Tokyo. The cost of one-way bus fare to Kawaguchiko from Shinjuku is around ¥1,250–¥2,000.

Here’s a quick list of things to do in Yokohama: - Minato Mirai: This waterfront area is perfect for shopping and dining. Check out the Landmark Tower for panoramic views. - Yokohama Chinatown: One of the largest Chinatowns in the world, it’s a great place to try some authentic Chinese food. - Sankeien Garden: A traditional Japanese garden that’s a peaceful escape from the city hustle.

Yokohama offers a nice blend of urban life and cultural experiences.

The Beauty of Kawaguchiko Under Mount Fuji

If I could only take one day trip from Tokyo, it would be to Kawaguchiko, the lake town that sits beneath Mount Fuji’s iconic peak. The views here are simply breathtaking.

Here’s what to do in Kawaguchiko: 1. Lake Kawaguchi: Rent a bike and ride around the lake for stunning views. 2. Oishi Park: This park offers some of the best photo ops of Mount Fuji, especially during cherry blossom season. 3. Kawaguchiko Music Forest Museum: A quirky spot with beautiful gardens and music-themed exhibits.

As Gladis Morales says, “If I could only take one day trip from Tokyo, it would be to Kawaguchiko.” It’s hard to argue with that!

Conclusion

  • Tokyo's day trips offer a mix of culture, history, and nature.
  • Each destination has its own unique vibe and attractions.
  • Plan your trips based on your interests to make the most of your time.

FAQ

How to get to Nikko from Tokyo?
Take the Tobu Nikko Line from Asakusa Station. It’s about a 2-hour ride, and you can enjoy beautiful scenery along the way.

What to do in Kamakura on a day trip?
Visit the Great Buddha, explore Hase-dera Temple, and stroll along Komachi Street for some delicious snacks. Don’t miss the sweet potato ice cream!

Is Hakone worth a day trip from Tokyo?
Absolutely! You can soak in hot springs, take a boat ride on Lake Ashi, and see stunning views of Mount Fuji. It’s a great way to unwind and enjoy nature.
